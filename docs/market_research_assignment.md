# OpenDiabetic Market Research Assignment

## Objective

Research how OpenDiabetic Foundation can become trusted public-good compute infrastructure for diabetic life, research, developers, AI agents, local support, and personal health vaults without monetizing or harvesting diabetic data.

## Major Research Section: Diabetic Compute Infrastructure Market

Research the following markets, technologies, and trust dynamics:

- Patient-owned health data
- Personal health records
- Local-first health apps
- Edge AI in healthcare
- Privacy-preserving compute
- Federated learning for healthcare
- Synthetic health data
- De-identified diabetes datasets
- Diabetes AI research tools
- Nonprofit compute foundations
- Developer ecosystems for healthcare
- Apple Health / HealthKit ecosystem
- Wearable notification workflows
- NAS/private cloud opportunities
- Open-source diabetes projects such as Nightscout and Tidepool
- Remote patient monitoring gaps
- Data privacy concerns among diabetics
- Why people may not trust platforms that monetize health data

## Deliverable 1: Compute Layer Strategy

Answer:

- What compute should OpenDiabetic own?
- What compute should run locally?
- What compute should run in the cloud?
- What should run on NAS?
- What should run on Jetson/edge appliances?
- What should run on phones/watches?
- What should never leave the user's control?

## Deliverable 2: Data Ownership Doctrine

Define doctrine for:

- No harvesting
- User-owned records
- Explicit consent
- Local-first defaults
- Optional sharing
- Research opt-in only
- De-identification standards
- Emergency access boundaries
- Family/caregiver permissions

## Deliverable 3: Developer Platform Strategy

Research and propose:

- SDK ideas
- API ideas
- DiabeticOS modules
- Agent templates
- Model evaluation harnesses
- Privacy guardrails
- Local-first app patterns
- Compute credits for builders

## Deliverable 4: Research Compute Strategy

Research and propose:

- Open research credits
- Nonprofit compute grants
- Model evaluation jobs
- Diabetic education model testing
- Privacy-preserving research
- Synthetic data generation
- Clinical partner boundaries
- Public-good compute ledger

## Deliverable 5: Lifestyle OS Strategy

Describe how DiabeticOS becomes the daily simplicity layer for people living with diabetes.

Focus on reminders, routines, supplies, food, foot care, doctor prep, family updates, emergency sheets, recovery, local support, dignity, reduced chaos, and reduced cognitive load.

## Deliverable 6: Foundation Model

Research nonprofit/public benefit structure options for a compute foundation that:

- Accepts donations
- Provides compute credits
- Supports diabetic users
- Supports researchers
- Supports developers
- Offers open-source tools
- Supports local care packs
- Avoids monetizing user health data

## Final Strategic Question

How does OpenDiabetic become the trusted compute foundation for diabetic life, research, and local support without becoming another health-data harvesting platform?
